ABOUT NAZARETH

Nazareth is a city in Israel with biblical history. In the old city, the domed Basilica of the Annunciation is, some believe,
where the angel Gabriel told Mary she would bear a child.
St. Joseph’s Church is said to be the site of Joseph’s carpentry workshop.
The underground Synagogue Church is reputedly where Jesus studied and prayed.
Nazareth Village, an open-air museum, reconstructs daily life in Jesus’ era.
Nazareth, historic city of Lower Galilee, in northern Israel;
it is the largest Arab city of the country.
In the New Testament Nazareth is associated with Jesus as his boyhood home,
and in its synagogue he preached the sermon that led to his rejection by his fellow townsmen.
Nazareth's location in Galilee placed it right in the middle of power struggles between different empires and kingdoms throughout history.
Romans, Byzantines, Arabs, Crusaders – they all fought for control of the region, and Nazareth likely changed hands numerous times.
This would have made for a very dynamic and sometimes turbulent history for the town.

Key Points

  1. Nazareth is a significant location for Christians, as it is believed to be the home of Mary and Joseph and the site of the Annunciation.
  2. The city is a major center of Christian pilgrimage, featuring sites like the Basilica of the Annunciation and St. Joseph's Church.
  3. Nazareth has a diverse population, including a significant number of Israeli Arabs, and it is known for its vibrant cultural scene.
  4. The city has a rich history, with archaeological sites dating back to the Bronze Age.



Visiting Nazareth

Visitors to Nazareth can explore its historical and religious sites, enjoy the local cuisine, and experience the unique blend of cultures. The Old City, with its narrow streets and bustling markets, offers a glimpse into the city's past and present.
